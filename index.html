Helping furniture last longer, with lower environmental impact

Forests play an essential role in maintaining ecological balance by supporting clean air, storing carbon, regulating water cycles, and protecting wildlife habitats. The furniture industry relies heavily on wood, which means the way materials are sourced and how long products remain in use are crucial factors affecting global forest health. This website explores how IKEA addresses challenges related to deforestation, furniture waste, and unsustainable consumption patterns. It also highlights how circular design principles help keep high‑quality products in use for longer, reducing environmental impact throughout their lifecycle.

IKEA’s approach focuses not only on responsible forestry but also on redesigning products and business models so that items can be reused, repaired, or recycled instead of thrown away. By combining efficient manufacturing, certified materials, and new customer services, IKEA shows how large retailers can reduce waste while empowering people to make more sustainable choices.

Context

Deforestation refers to the large‑scale removal of trees from forested land, often resulting in long‑term ecological damage. Forests provide oxygen, support biodiversity, protect soil from erosion, and store significant amounts of carbon, making them key in mitigating climate change. When logging practices are poorly managed, or when wood is taken from vulnerable regions, ecosystems become less resilient and communities who depend on forests can be negatively affected.

Because wood is one of the most widely used raw materials in the furniture sector, poorly regulated harvesting can accelerate habitat loss. Responsible sourcing, certification, and sustainable forest management are critical to reducing these risks and ensuring forests remain healthy and productive.

IKEA at a glance

IKEA is one of the world’s largest and most recognisable furniture retailers, supplying millions of households with affordable home solutions. Given its global scale, the company’s decisions on sourcing, product design, and waste reduction have a significant impact across the industry. IKEA’s circular strategy prioritises extending the lifespan of products by making them easier to reuse, repair, refurbish, and recycle, creating a more efficient and less wasteful system.

FY25 results show that 96.5% of all wood used in IKEA products was FSC‑certified or sourced from recycled material. Approximately 19% of total wood use came from recycled content, showcasing ongoing progress towards more sustainable material choices.
