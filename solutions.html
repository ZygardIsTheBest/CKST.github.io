What IKEA is doing now

Actions to reduce deforestation risks and furniture waste while making the business more circular.

Current actions

Wood sourcing and recycled content: IKEA’s FY25 performance shows that 96.5% of all wood used is either FSC‑certified or recycled, with around 19% from recycled sources. IKEA’s 2030 ambition is that at least one‑third of the wood‑based range will be made from recycled wood.

Plastics: By 2030, all plastic used in IKEA products will come from renewable or recycled sources.

Circular design goals: By 2030, products should be designed to be reused, repaired, refurbished and recycled, with recycled and renewable content increased to at least 90%.

Circular customer services (UK example): “Buyback & Resell” enables customers to return eligible products for store credit; items in good condition are resold in Re‑shop & Re‑use areas.

Operational energy (Ingka): FY25 operations matched 94.8% of electricity with renewable sources.

What IKEA could improve

  1. Improve transparency and traceability (audits, corrective actions, higher‑risk areas).
  2. Make repairs easier than replacement (spare parts, instructions, standardised components).
  3. Scale circular services globally (buy‑back, refurbishment, reverse logistics, incentives).

How customers can reduce furniture waste

  • Select second‑hand items or shop within Re‑shop & Re‑use areas.
  • Use IKEA buyback and resale services as an alternative to disposal.
  • Repair furniture where possible by replacing small parts.
  • Donate or resell items locally.
  • Recycle materials correctly by separating wood, metal and plastic components.
