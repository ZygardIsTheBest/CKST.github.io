The issue: deforestation and furniture waste

Global context

Environmental Impact: Deforestation & Waste from the Furniture Industry

Deforestation means cutting down large areas of forest so the land has fewer trees than before. Forests are important because they clean the air, support many animals and plants, store carbon, and protect soil. When forests are cut down unsustainably, there are serious effects on the environment. The furniture industry uses a lot of wood, so it is connected to deforestation when wood is not sourced responsibly.

Wood is one of the main raw materials used to make furniture like tables, chairs, and cabinets. Some studies estimate the furniture sector accounts for a large share of global wood consumption. Because of this demand, forests must be logged — and if the wood is not certified or managed well, this contributes to forest loss and environmental harm.

Environmental consequences

  • Loss of biodiversity – many plants, insects and animals lose habitat and may become endangered.
  • Carbon emissions – cutting trees releases stored carbon and worsens climate change.
  • Soil degradation – without roots, rain can wash soil away and cause erosion.

Furniture waste

Every year, large volumes of furniture are discarded by households and businesses. Across regions like the United States and Europe, millions of tonnes of furniture are thrown away annually, with most ending up in landfills. Globally, furniture waste is a growing problem because many products are made from mixed materials (wood, metal, plastic), making them hard to recycle or reuse. Reasons include short product lifetimes, low recycling and fast production cycles.

IKEA’s role and scrutiny

IKEA is one of the world’s largest furniture retailers, so its material demand (especially wood) makes it a key actor in the deforestation and furniture-waste conversation. IKEA reports that in FY25, 96.5% of the total wood it used (for products and indirect materials like packaging) was either FSC-certified or recycled, and around 19% was recycled wood. Reuters and NGOs have also scrutinised supply-chain risks in regions such as Ukraine and Romania, prompting audits and renewed focus on traceability.
